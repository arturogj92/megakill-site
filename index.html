How it works · 3 shots

Force-quitting that actually feels good.

01 SUMMON

Hit the shortcut

Hold your modifier combo (⌥⌘ by default). The cursor turns into a shotgun over any screen. You're armed.

02 AIM

Pick your target

Aim at the app you want gone: its Dock icon, or its window directly. Yes, that browser with 38 tabs that's been open for three weeks eating your RAM.

03 EXECUTE

Fire

BOOM. Instant force-quit, the shotgun blast roars, and if it was pinned it gets unpinned: the icon vanishes from the Dock for good.

Wound mode

Turn it on and a shot on a window closes just that window — the app stays alive (perfect for that floating Slack chat). A shot on the Dock icon still kills the whole app.

Pinned cleanup

A closed app still pinned to the Dock? One shot and it's unpinned — the leftover icon is gone for good.

The arsenal

Made to clear your Dock the loud way.

DOOM shotgun

A real DOOM '93-style shotgun sprite (CC0 assets). Recoil, muzzle flash and the exact id Software feel.

Real sounds

Real fire and reload sounds, not system beeps. Every kill is heard. Everyone in the open-plan will hear it. Good.

Configurable shortcut

Summon it with any modifier combo you like. See app, shoot app, app gone, in under a second. No menus.

Safety guard

Finder, the Dock and MEGAKILL are untouchable. It only shoots running apps; critical system processes are shielded.
